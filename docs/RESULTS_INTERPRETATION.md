# Results interpretation

## Simple reading

KCNQ1 and KCNE3 are much more frequently detected in intestinal-like malignant epithelial cells than in diffuse/EMT-like cells. KCNE2 follows the same direction, but with fewer detected cells and less consistent continuous-score evidence.

## Quantitative results

| Gene | Intestinal detection | Diffuse/EMT detection | Fisher odds ratio | Adjusted p-value |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| KCNQ1 | 25.66% | 1.66% | 20.39 | 7.07e-178 |
| KCNE2 | 4.12% | 0.79% | 5.40 | 3.46e-17 |
| KCNE3 | 28.15% | 5.74% | 6.43 | 1.35e-122 |

An odds ratio above one means that detection is more likely in intestinal-like cells in the Fisher exact test.

KCNQ1 and KCNE3 also retain positive within-patient correlations with the intestinal score and negative correlations with the diffuse/EMT score. KCNE2 retains the negative diffuse/EMT association, but its within-patient intestinal correlation is close to zero and not significant.

## Biological interpretation

The strongest conclusion is a transcriptional-state association: KCNQ1 and KCNE3 are enriched in malignant epithelial cells that preserve an intestinal and epithelial program, whereas they are depleted as the diffuse/EMT program increases. This is compatible with a more differentiated and junction-preserving epithelial state, but the analysis cannot determine whether channel expression maintains that state or simply reflects it.

KCNE2 is lowly detected and should be described cautiously. Its detection difference is supported, but the continuous intestinal association is not robust within patients. A reasonable formulation is that KCNE2 shows an intestinal-oriented detection pattern that requires confirmation.

## Implications for the project

The next useful validations are RNAscope or spatial transcriptomics to verify localization, protein-level measurement because RNA does not guarantee a functional channel complex, and functional perturbation of KCNQ1/KCNE subunits in gastric epithelial models followed by epithelial-junction and EMT readouts.
